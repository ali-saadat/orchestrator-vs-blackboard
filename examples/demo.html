The Party Problem 🎉

😀Guestswrites the guest list
💰Budgetwatches the money
🍕Foodorders the pizzas
🎵Vibepicks the party style

Four friends plan a birthday party. Each friend has one job.

😀 Guests wants 15 people on the list.

💰 Budget has $600. One guest costs $50 — that pays for their food, drinks and cake.

15 guests × $50 = $750. That is more than $600. Problem! 😬

🍕 Food must know the final list: they order 1 pizza for every 3 guests.

🎵 Vibe must know it too: many people = a big wild party, few people = a small chill one.

So one change moves everything. The friends must talk until the whole plan fits the money.

✅ They have $600❌ They need $750

Three ways to talk 💬

Same friends. Same problem. Three ways to talk together.

👔 The Boss Way (Orchestrator)

One boss asks each friend. One by one. Again and again. At the end, the boss asks everyone one more time, just to check.

📋 The Whiteboard Way (Blackboard)

All friends share one whiteboard. When a number changes, only the right friends speak again.

🤝 The Mix Way (Hybrid)

Guests and Budget fix the money problem first, together. Then Food and Vibe speak once.

⭐ All three find the same party. But more talk costs more time and more money.

Your guess: which way needs the least talk?
